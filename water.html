SYSTEM 2 - WATER

Water is the first system to become critical

Most households in the suburban jungle store weeks of food but often less than a single day of water. Water systems are centralized and invisible, which makes their failure sudden and disruptive. The math is unforgiving.

What This System Does

Overview

At a bare survival minimum, plan 0.5 gallons per person per day just for drinking. Once you add cooking and basic hygiene, a realistic baseline is 2.5 gallons per person per day. A family of four burns through 10 gallons a day. In three days, that's 30 gallons.
Municipal water feels infinite until it isn't. Pumping stations need electricity. Main breaks happen. Contamination triggers boil notices. And if you're on a well, "water" is actually an energy problem - no power means no pump means no water, regardless of how much is in the ground.
Unlike energy, water has hard biological limits. You can dim the lights, but you can't dim your kidneys.
The fundamental question: If your faucets stopped flowing right now, how many days could your household sustain itself on water alone?
Two-Minute Assessment

Pre-Flight Check

Every household water system answers three simple questions:
  1. How much water do you have right now?
  2. How will you get more when that runs out?
  3. How will you make new water safe to drink?
That's the whole system. Storage answers the first question. Sources and Transfer answer the second. Purification and Filtration answer the third.

Calculate Your Water Independence Score

1) Daily requirement
People: (household size) x 2.5 gallons = daily requirement
Pets: add 1 gallon per pet per day (as a planning number)
That's your daily burn rate.
2) Stored water on hand (today)
How many gallons do you have filled and ready right now?
3) Water Independence Score (days)
Stored gallons / daily requirement = days
That number is your current Water Independence Score. For most households, it's less than one.

Interpretation Guide

Days of Independence Your Situation
0-1 days Fully dependent on continuous municipal supply
3 days Can ride out short service interruptions
7 days Resilient to most local infrastructure failures
14+ days No longer operating on a countdown
∞ (Infinity) Independence depends on access, not storage

The Binary Question

Critical Dependency Check
Do you have any way to treat water from questionable sources?
If YES (source + transfer + purification + filtration methods in place), then stored water is a bridge to a renewable supply. Your score stops being capped by container size.
If NO, your independence has a hard ceiling. When the last container is empty, you're done.
Well Water Dependency
If you're on well water, your water supply depends on your Energy system. No power potentially means no pump means no water. If your Energy Independence Score is zero, your Water Independence Score may be zero too. These systems are often linked.
Capability 1 of 6

Water Storage

Storage sets the floor of your water system's independence score. Storage buys time. Time to wait for service restoration. Time to identify alternate water sources. Time to deploy your backup capabilities. Without stored water, you're forced to solve the hardest problems immediately.
Baseline planning number: a family of four at 2.5 gallons per person per day needs 30 gallons for 72 hours. That fits in a corner of your basement. For reference, most hot water heaters hold 40-50 gallons.
Use food-grade containers for all water storage.
Caution
Non-food-grade containers can leach chemicals, degrade quickly, and promote bacterial growth.
Water Storage Protocol
  1. Fill containers from your tap
  2. Add a small amount of unscented household bleach (5-8% sodium hypochlorite) for long-term storage
  3. Seal tightly
  4. Label with the fill date
  5. Store cool, dark, and temperature-stable (basement or ground floor)
  6. Avoid garages and direct sunlight

Stored tap water that's treated and sealed properly lasts months. Commercial preservers extend that to years. The important thing is having water on hand before you need it.

Capability 2 of 6

Alternate Water Sources

Water storage sets the floor of your independence score. Alternate water sources remove the ceiling. When your stored water is depleted, your independence does not have to end. It transitions. Your system stops depending on what you stored and starts depending on what you can access, transfer, purify, and filter.
In the suburban jungle, alternate water sources are everywhere. Most people simply never notice them because they've never needed to. Your job is not to create water. Your job is to recognize where it already exists and ensure you can safely use it.

Source Hierarchy: Safest to Most Conditional

1
Nearby Buildings with Pressurized Water
The most reliable and overlooked alternate source. During localized outages, water often remains available in nearby structures due to differences in elevation, pressure zones, or infrastructure routing. Schools, hospitals, office buildings, and neighboring homes may retain pressure even when your home does not. Water utilities rarely fail uniformly. Failures occur in sections. Your home may be dry while a building two blocks away has fully functioning faucets. This is why transfer capability matters.
2
Hot Water Heaters
Every suburban home already contains a built-in emergency water reserve. Most hot water heaters hold 40 to 50 gallons of clean, potable water. For a family of four, that's roughly four to five days already inside your home. This water remains usable even if municipal pressure is lost.
3
Swimming Pools and Spas
An average residential pool contains 10,000 to 20,000 gallons. This water is not immediately drinkable - it contains chlorine and environmental contaminants - but it is fully usable after proper purification and filtration. A single neighborhood pool contains enough water to support hundreds of households for extended periods. Access and treatment determine usability.
4
Rainwater
Your roof is a large collection surface. Even light rainfall produces meaningful volumes. Rainwater must always be filtered and purified before drinking, but it provides a renewable source that does not depend on infrastructure. During an outage, a bucket under your gutter's downspout is a viable collection method if you have filtration and purification capability.
5
Surface Water (Last Resort)
Rivers, streams, ponds, and lakes provide abundant water but represent the highest biological risk. Surface water must always be purified and filtered before use. This requires the most complete capability chain. Not your primary plan - your contingency plan.
Note on Hot Water Heaters
Turn off the heat source to the tank before draining. Open faucets to relieve pressure.
Capability 3 of 6

Water Transfer

Once an alternate water source is identified, the next challenge is getting it where you need it. Transfer capability is water logistics: containers, pumps, and simple tools that turn alternate sources into accessible supply.
Water Transfer Protocol

This protocol governs how water moves from alternate sources into your clean drinking supply. The objective: prevent contamination from crossing into your clean system.

Recommend four total jerry cans: two permanently designated for untreated water, two permanently designated for purified water. Color-code them - one color for dirty, one for clean. This creates a visible decontamination boundary that prevents mistakes under stress.

Follow these steps in order:

  1. Collect source water using designated dirty-water cans only
  2. Strain sediment using cloth or pre-filter if water is visibly cloudy
  3. Chemically purify water in dirty-water containers using Aquatabs or bleach
  4. Transfer purified water into designated clean-water cans
  5. Feed purified water from clean cans into your gravity filtration system
  6. Dispense filtered water into drinking containers

Contamination moves in one direction only: toward purification, never backward into clean storage. With proper handling discipline, your system remains safe, scalable, and renewable indefinitely.

Pumps and Dispensing

A full 5-gallon container weighs over 40 pounds. Tipping, pouring, and funneling repeatedly is awkward and inefficient. Over time, this friction discourages proper rotation and use. Every household should have a siphon pump. A shaker siphon is the lowest-cost, highest-reliability option - a clear hose with a check valve. Capability 4 of 6

Water Purification

Storage sets the floor of your Water Independence Score. Alternate sources remove the ceiling. Purification is what makes alternate sources usable.
Without purification capability, alternate water sources are just potential. With it, they become supply.
Warning
Untreated source water can turn a manageable situation into a medical emergency. Giardia, cryptosporidium, bacteria, and viruses cause severe gastrointestinal illness - which accelerates dehydration, which is the thing that can actually kill you. If you're not certain water is safe, treat it.
Most alternate water sources contain biological contamination - bacteria, viruses, and parasites that can cause severe gastrointestinal illness. In an emergency, this is not an inconvenience. It is a system failure. Illness accelerates dehydration, impairs decision-making, and creates medical problems when medical care may be unavailable.
Purification eliminates biological threats. This is the step that converts unsafe water into usable water. Purification always happens before filtration and before water enters your clean storage containers.

Method 1: Chemical Purification (Primary)

Chemical purification is the most reliable and scalable method for households in the suburban jungle. No electricity, minimal equipment, works at any scale.
Unscented Household Bleach
Unscented household bleach (5-8% sodium hypochlorite) is extremely effective at killing bacteria, viruses, and parasites. Inexpensive, widely available, stores for years.
If in doubt, fill a toothpaste cap with bleach and add it to a gallon of water. Stir thoroughly. Wait 30 minutes. Should smell faintly of chlorine. If not, repeat and wait another 30 minutes. Every household should have bleach available as a purification fallback.

Method 2: Boiling (Secondary)

Boiling is the most reliable purification method ever discovered. Heat destroys bacteria, viruses, and parasites completely.
Bring water to a rolling boil. Maintain for one minute. Allow to cool naturally.
Boiling does not remove sediment, heavy metals, or chemical contaminants - strain cloudy water first and use your gravity filter afterward when possible. Think of boiling as a biological reset. It makes water safe to drink. Filtration makes it safe to live on long-term.
The limitation is dependency on energy. Every gallon you boil is fuel you're not using for cooking or heating. This makes boiling an excellent backup method but not the most scalable primary method for sustained operations.

Water Filtration

Purification makes water biologically safe. Filtration makes it operationally safe. These are not the same thing.
Chemical purification and boiling neutralize bacteria, viruses, and parasites. But they do not remove sediment, particulates, chlorine taste, or environmental contaminants. Filtration does.
Think of purification as sterilization. Think of filtration as refinement. Purification allows you to drink water without getting sick. Filtration allows you to live on that water indefinitely.
Filtration is the final step before water enters your drinking supply. It always happens in the clean zone.

Gravity Filtration System

Gravity filters are the backbone of a renewable household water system. No electricity, no plumbing, no pressure. Pour water into the upper chamber and gravity pulls it through filter elements into the lower chamber. Recommend installing your gravity filter in the kitchen and leaving it there permanently. This keeps your clean zone physically separated from your dirty zone.

Capability 6 of 6

Water Use

Equipment without knowledge is inventory. Knowledge turns inventory into capability.
Conservation is capability multiplication. Reduce consumption by 25% and you extend endurance by 25%. Cut consumption in half and you double endurance. Water discipline is math.

Rationing Protocol (Priority Order)

  1. Drinking - non-negotiable. Everyone gets minimum intake before a gallon goes anywhere else.
  2. Cooking - when water is limited, your food system is affected. Canned goods at room temperature require zero water. Peanut butter sandwiches require zero water. Pasta, rice, and freeze-dried meals all cost water. Plan menus around conservation.
  3. Hygiene - wipes, washcloths, and basins handle most needs.
  4. Sanitation - a standard toilet flush uses 1.6 gallons. Six flushes a day across a family of four is 38 gallons - nearly four days of drinking water. See the Sanitation system for a full discussion on recycling gray water.

Bug-Out Water Kit

Free Tip
Add Pedialyte packets or Liquid IV to your pantry now. In a water-limited situation, maximizing what your body absorbs from each glass matters more than total volume. Electrolytes make hydration more efficient, and they make kids more willing to drink.
The Math

A Note on Bottled Water

The most common water strategy in the suburban jungle is buying bottled water when the news says a storm is coming.
A case of 24 bottles is roughly 3 gallons. A family of four needs 10 gallons per day. That's more than three cases daily just to meet minimum requirements. For 72 hours, you need ten cases. That assumes the store has stock, you get there first, and you have the trunk space. At $6 per case, that's $60 for water you could have stored for a fraction of the cost.
Bottled water is fine for grab-and-go and evacuation. It is not a water system. All a typical family needs to actually be ready is about $150 in baseline equipment, staged in advance.

Where It Lives

Configuration

Water is heavy. A gallon weighs over eight pounds. Thirty gallons weighs 250 pounds. Where you store it matters.
  • Primary storage: Cool, dark, stable temperature, accessible to the household
  • AquaPods: In the bathroom where they'll be deployed
  • Treatment supplies: Stored with water storage (one station, one location)
  • Transfer gear: Near your exit point (garage or mudroom)
  • Portable filtration: In your go-bag or emergency cabinet
Accessibility beats optimization. Water you can't reach is water you won't use.
Keep It Ready

Maintenance

Water is the easiest system to let slide and the most dangerous one to neglect. Stored water doesn't look like it's degrading. Containers don't announce leaks until they leak.

Build It Into Existing Habits

  • Rotate bottled water as part of groceries
  • Quick visual check of containers when you check smoke detectors
  • Confirm AquaPods are still staged when you clean bathrooms

Once a Year: Use the Hour You Get Back

When Daylight Saving Time ends in November, spend part of that hour on your water system:
  • Rotate stored water
  • Inspect and replace filter elements as needed
  • Check tablet expiration dates
  • Replace AquaPod liners
  • Inspect containers for cracks, discoloration, and degradation
Maintenance preserves capability, not equipment.
